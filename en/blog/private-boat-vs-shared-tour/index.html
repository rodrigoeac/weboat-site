Private Boat vs Shared Tour in Rio: Which to Choose?

When planning a boat trip in Rio de Janeiro, the first big decision is: rent a private boat or buy seats on a shared tour? The answer depends on the size of your group, your budget, and the type of experience you want. But here's the spoiler: for most groups, a private trip isn't just better -- it's also cheaper per person.

In this complete guide, we'll compare both options in detail: prices, what's included, freedom, privacy, and who each option works best for. With real numbers and a side-by-side comparison so you can decide with confidence.

What Is a Shared Boat Tour?

A shared tour works like a "maritime bus": you buy an individual seat and board alongside other people you don't know. It's the most common model offered by tourism platforms and travel agencies in Rio.

Here are the typical features of a shared boat tour:

  • Price: R$ 250 to R$ 350 per person (~$50-70 USD) (fixed price, regardless of group size)
  • Duration: 2 to 3 hours
  • Route: fixed, decided by the operator -- usually Arraial do Cabo or a basic route through Guanabara Bay
  • Schedule: fixed, no flexibility -- you adapt to the boat's timetable
  • Company: you share the space with strangers (between 10 and 30 people, depending on the vessel)
  • Music: controlled by the operator
  • Food: generally not included or limited to basic snacks
  • Stops: pre-set, no customization

The shared tour has its place: it works well for solo travelers, couples, or very small groups (3-4 people) who don't want to commit to renting an entire boat.

What Is a Private Boat Trip?

On a private trip, you rent the entire boat for your group. Nobody else boards -- the boat, the skipper, the time, and the route are all yours. This is the model offered by WeBoat Brasil, departing from Marina da Gloria.

Here's what you get on a private WeBoat trip:

  • Price: starting at R$ 2,300 (~$460 USD) for the entire boat (split among your group)
  • Duration: 5 full hours
  • Route: customizable -- choose from 6 routes or design a custom itinerary
  • Schedule: flexible -- you choose the departure time (morning or afternoon)
  • Company: only your group (friends, family, coworkers)
  • Music: Bluetooth sound system -- connect your phone and play your own playlist
  • Food: you can bring whatever you want (BYO) or book BBQ on board, open bar, cheese & charcuterie board
  • Stops: you decide where to stop, how long to stay, and when to move on
  • Included extras: fuel, professional skipper, coolers, floating mat, pool noodles, life jackets, insurance, on-board BBQ grill, and bathroom

Price Comparison: The Math That Changes Everything

Here's the crucial point of the comparison. The shared tour charges a fixed price per person. The private trip charges a total for the entire boat, which you split among the group. This means that the more people, the lower the per-person cost on a private trip -- until it becomes cheaper than the shared tour.

What You Get with Each Option

Beyond price, the experience is completely different. Here's a detailed comparison:

Freedom and Route

On a shared tour, the route is fixed. The boat departs at a set time, makes scheduled stops, and returns. You have no say in the decision. On a private trip, you choose the route: want to head to Mureta da Urca for a beer on the water? Want to stop at Ilhas Cagarras for snorkeling? Want to stay anchored at Praia Vermelha for 2 hours? You decide.

Privacy

On a shared tour, you share the space with strangers. Private conversations, taking photos freely, dancing, and games are all limited. On a private trip, the boat belongs to your group. You can celebrate a birthday, host a bachelorette party, or simply relax without anyone watching.

Time on the Water

The typical shared tour lasts 2 to 3 hours. A private WeBoat trip lasts 5 hours. That means more time on the water, more stops, more swimming, and less rushing. With 5 hours, you can enjoy a complete route at a relaxed pace.

Food and Drinks

On a shared tour, food options are very limited. On a private trip, you have three options: bring everything yourself (BYO -- no extra cost), book a BBQ on board with a professional grill master (starting at R$ 100/person, ~$20 USD), or choose an all inclusive package with open bar and cheese & charcuterie board. The boat has a BBQ grill, coolers, and space for everything.

Music and Atmosphere

On a shared tour, the music is the operator's choice. On a private trip, you connect your phone via Bluetooth and play whatever you want -- from Brazilian sertanejo to funk, from jazz to electronic. The volume and playlist are all yours.

When a Shared Tour Makes Sense

The shared tour isn't for everyone, but it has its place. It makes sense when:

  • You're traveling solo or as a couple and don't have a group to split a boat rental
  • Your group has fewer than 5 people and the per-person budget can't handle R$ 400+ (~$80+ USD)
  • You just want to try a boat trip without committing to a route or duration
  • The destination is Arraial do Cabo or another distant location where chartering costs are high
  • Strict budget: if each person can only spend up to R$ 300 (~$60 USD) and the group has fewer than 8 people

When a Private Trip Is the Best Choice

For the vast majority of situations in Rio de Janeiro, the private trip wins. It's the best choice when:

  • Your group has 8 or more people -- the per-person price is already lower than the shared tour
  • You're celebrating something -- birthdays, bachelorette parties, graduations, proposals
  • Family with children -- privacy, safety, and total control of the environment
  • Corporate event -- team building, company party, product launch (see corporate options)
  • You want total freedom -- choose the route, schedule, music, food, and pace of the trip
  • You want more time on the water -- 5 hours private vs 2-3 hours shared
  • You want add-on services -- BBQ, open bar, decoration, cheese board -- options only available on private trips
  • International tourists -- an exclusive, personalized experience without depending on fixed schedules

The Verdict: For Most Groups, Private Is Better

The conclusion is clear: if you have 8 people or more, a private boat trip in Rio de Janeiro offers a better per-person price, more than double the time, a custom route, total privacy, and the freedom to eat, drink, and listen to whatever you want. It's not just a better experience -- it's also more economical.

Even with smaller groups (5-7 people), the per-person cost on a private trip can be worth it when you consider that you're buying 5 hours of exclusivity instead of 2 hours shared with strangers.
