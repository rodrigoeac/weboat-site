How Much Does a Boat Trip Cost in Rio de Janeiro (2026)

"How much does a boat trip cost in Rio?" is the question we get asked the most -- and the answer might surprise you. A 5-hour private boat trip for up to 15 people starts at R$ 2,300 (approximately $460 USD), which means less than R$ 154 per person (~$31 USD). That's right: less than many concert tickets or a group dinner at a nice restaurant.

In this complete and up-to-date guide for 2026, we'll show you all prices, what's included, available add-on services, and how to calculate the per-person cost for your group. With full transparency -- because we believe clear information helps you make the best decision.

How Much Per Person? The Math That Changes Everything

The great advantage of a private trip is that the price is split among the group. Here's the breakdown for the WeBoat 32 (Monday to Thursday, R$ 2,300):

  • 5 people: R$ 460/person (~$92 USD)
  • 8 people: R$ 288/person (~$58 USD)
  • 10 people: R$ 230/person (~$46 USD)
  • 12 people: R$ 192/person (~$38 USD)
  • 15 people (max capacity): R$ 154/person (~$31 USD)
With 15 people on the WeBoat 32 (Monday to Thursday), each person pays only R$ 154 (~$31 USD) for a 5-hour private boat trip. Compare that with shared tours that charge R$ 250-350 per person on a fixed route with strangers.

For the Rio Star 50 on weekends (R$ 4,500, up to 22 people), the per-person cost drops to R$ 205 (~$41 USD) -- on a 50-foot boat with plenty of space for a party.

Weekday vs Weekend: How to Save Money

The difference between Monday-Thursday and Friday-Sunday ranges from R$ 400 to R$ 500 per boat. If your group has schedule flexibility, booking on a weekday is the easiest way to save.

Other ways to reduce costs:

  • Bigger group: per-person cost drops dramatically. 15 people on WeBoat 32 = R$ 154/person vs 5 people = R$ 460/person
  • BYO (Bring Your Own): bring your own food and drinks instead of booking add-on services
  • Closer route: the Mureta da Urca route is the most affordable, starting at R$ 2,300
  • Book early: reserve in advance to secure your preferred date and avoid peak season pricing

Private Boat vs Shared Tour: Which Is Better Value?

In Rio de Janeiro, there are two types of boat experiences: private (you rent the entire boat for your group) and shared (you buy individual seats on a tour with strangers).

  • Shared tour: R$ 250-350 per person, fixed route, fixed schedule, with strangers, 2-3 hours duration
  • Private (WeBoat): from R$ 154/person (15 people on WeBoat 32), custom route, your schedule, 5 hours, your group only

The math is simple: with 8-10 or more people, a private trip is actually cheaper than a shared tour -- with far more freedom, privacy, and time on the water.

Frequently Asked Questions About Prices

Do prices change throughout the year?

Base prices remain stable year-round. The exception is New Year's Eve, when high demand may affect pricing. For Carnival and holiday weekends, we recommend booking in advance.

Can I extend the trip beyond 5 hours?

Yes. Additional hours are charged proportionally. Contact us via WhatsApp for rates.

If it rains, do I lose my money?

No. In case of adverse weather conditions, the trip is rescheduled at no cost. Your booking and payment are guaranteed for a new date. Learn more in our FAQ page.

Is tipping expected?

Tipping is not mandatory but is a common and appreciated practice. Amounts between R$ 100 and R$ 300 are typical, split among the group.
